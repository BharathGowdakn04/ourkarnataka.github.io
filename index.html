Our Mission

NAMMA KARNATAKA

Karnataka, a diverse state in South India, boasts the historic city of Mysuru with its opulent palaces, including the iconic Mysore Palace. The lush landscapes of Coorg, known as the "Scotland of India," offer serene coffee plantations and mist-covered hills. Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, showcases the ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire, creating a mesmerizing blend of history and architecture.

MYSORE

Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is an opulent royal residence in Mysuru, Karnataka, India. Built in Indo-Saracenic style, the palace mesmerizes visitors with its intricate architecture, ornate interiors, and the grand Durbar Hall. Illuminated during the annual Dasara festival, it stands as a symbol of Mysuru's rich cultural heritage.

Murdeshwara

Murdeshwara Shiva Temple, located in the coastal town of Murdeshwar in Karnataka, India, is renowned for its colossal statue of Lord Shiva, standing at 123 feet. Situated along the Arabian Sea, the temple complex offers breathtaking ocean views, and its architecture exemplifies a harmonious blend of traditional and modern styles. The temple is a significant pilgrimage site and a popular tourist destination.
