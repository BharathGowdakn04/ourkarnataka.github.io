History Of KARNATAKA

Karnataka, located in the southwestern part of India, boasts a rich and diverse history that spans millennia. The region has been a cradle of ancient civilizations, with evidence of early human settlements dating back to the Stone Age. The classical period saw the flourishing of powerful dynasties such as the Mauryas, Satavahanas, and Kadambas. However, it was during the medieval era that Karnataka witnessed its golden age under the mighty Vijayanagara Empire, ruled by the illustrious Krishnadevaraya. The Deccan Sultanates and the Bahmani Kingdom also left their imprint on the state's history. In the 18th century, Karnataka became a battleground for European powers, with the British ultimately establishing their dominance. Post-independence, Karnataka emerged as a significant state in the Indian Union, contributing substantially to technology, education, and cultural diversity. The historical sites like Hampi, Badami, and the temples of Belur and Halebid bear testimony to Karnataka's rich and enduring heritage.


Our Mission

The mission for Karnataka's heritage involves conserving historical sites, promoting cultural awareness, and fostering community engagement to ensure the preservation and sustainable promotion of the state's rich and diverse legacy. Legislative measures, tourism development, and technological integration further contribute to this overarching mission.
